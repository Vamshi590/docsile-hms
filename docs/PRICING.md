## Why these plans are priced this way

The fee is not for "software" — it's for the outcomes each tier unlocks. Below is exactly what each plan delivers, with concrete examples from how hospitals use it day-to-day.

### 🟢 Basic — ₹50,000 setup + ₹10,000 / 6 months

**For:** single-location hospitals that want to digitise operations end-to-end without overpaying for AI.

**You get all 16 modules of the HMS:**

1. **Dashboard** — daily snapshot of footfall, revenue, pending dues, and alerts.
2. **Patients (OPD)** — registration, visit history, demographics, search.
3. **Refraction / Workup** — optometrist eye-readings, AR, present & previous glass prescription, clinical findings.
4. **Doctor Console** — consultation, prescription, investigations, follow-up scheduling.
5. **In-Patients (IPD)** — admission, OT, payments, package billing, discharge summary.
6. **Pharmacy** — full inventory, purchase orders, supplier ledger, billing, stock alerts, expiry tracking, batch-wise FIFO.
7. **Optical** — frames & lenses inventory, prescription-linked billing, stock.
8. **Labs** — investigations, lab bills, payments, result entry.
9. **Insurance** — claim creation, status tracking, TPA-wise reports.
10. **Dues & Follow-Ups** — outstanding balances across OPD/IPD/Pharmacy/Optical/Lab + scheduled follow-ups in one place.
11. **Expenses** — categorised expense entry, vendor tracking, monthly P&L view.
12. **Analytics** — region, doctor, service, package, and trend analytics (covered below).
13. **Reports** — exportable financial and operational reports for accountants and management.
14. **Data Export** — bulk CSV/Excel exports for audit, GST, or migration.
15. **License Tracker** — hospital licenses, drug schedules, fire/pollution NOCs with expiry alerts.
16. **Staff** — attendance, shifts, role-based permissions, audit trail.

**The headline feature here is Analytics.** Most HMS vendors stop at "we have a dashboard". Docsile gives you a real decision-making surface.

#### Examples of how Analytics pays for itself

1. **Region-wise patient flow**
   See exactly which mandals, villages, or pin codes your patients are coming from. If 38% of last quarter's cataract patients came from a single mandal that you've never advertised in, that's where your next outreach camp goes. One well-targeted camp typically brings in 30–60 surgeries — far more than the ₹10k six-month fee.

2. **Doctor-wise revenue & conversion**
   For each consulting doctor: OPD footfall, surgery conversion rate, average revenue per patient, and follow-up adherence. If Dr. A converts 22% of consults into surgeries and Dr. B converts 8%, you know who needs more clinic time and who needs a counsellor sitting next to them.

3. **Service-mix and pricing leakage**
   Which services are billed most? Which are discounted most? Are your front-desk staff under-charging certain packages? Analytics surfaces it without you having to audit a single bill manually.

4. **Inventory turn & expiry risk**
   Pharmacy and optical stock that hasn't moved in 90 days, items nearing expiry, and slow-selling frames. Saves 1–2% of inventory write-offs every quarter.

5. **Insurance claim health**
   Pending / approved / rejected ratios, average TAT per insurance company, and money stuck in pipeline. One unblocked ₹3L claim recovers six months of fees.

**Why this price:** ₹50k setup covers migration + training; ₹10k / 6 months keeps the lights on (hosting, backups, support, free upgrades). It's deliberately the lowest barrier so smaller hospitals don't have to choose between paper and a real system.

### 🟣 Premier — ₹50,000 setup + ₹10,000 / month

**For:** hospitals that want to grow their patient base, not just digitise it. Same setup fee as Basic — the difference is the monthly fee, because AI and social automation work *every day*.

**Includes everything in Basic, plus:**

#### Sitha AI — your in-house clinical & administrative co-pilot

Sitha AI is contextual — it knows your patients, your prescriptions, your IPD admissions. Examples:

1. **Patient summarisation in the doctor's console**
   Before a follow-up, Sitha gives the doctor a 4-line summary: prior diagnosis, last medications, last visit findings, and outstanding payments. Cuts patient-prep time from 3 minutes to 15 seconds. Across 60 consults/day, that's ~3 hours of doctor time saved daily — directly translatable to more patients seen.

2. **Discharge summary drafting**
   Doctor enters the procedure and condition; Sitha drafts the discharge note from the patient's IPD timeline (admission, OT, post-op, medications). Doctor reviews and signs. What used to be a 15-minute task is now 2 minutes.

3. **Smart prescription suggestions**
   Pulls from past prescription templates and current patient context. Especially useful for junior doctors and residents — reduces prescription errors and standardises practice across the hospital.

4. **Front-desk Q&A**
   "How many cataracts last week from Madanapalle?" → instant answer, no Excel.

5. **Monthly Financial Health Report**
   On the 1st of every month, Sitha generates a 1-page financial review of the previous month and emails it to the owner / administrator. It tells you, in plain English:
   - **Where revenue grew or dropped** — e.g., *"OPD revenue is up 12% MoM, but IPD package revenue dropped 18% — driven mainly by fewer phaco surgeries from Chittoor mandal. Consider a camp there."*
   - **Where to focus to grow** — *"Optical conversion from refraction patients dropped from 41% to 28%. A counsellor on Wednesdays could recover ~₹1.4L/month."*
   - **Where to cut expenses** — *"Pharmacy expiry write-offs were ₹38,000 last month, up from ₹11,000. Three SKUs (listed) account for 70% — reduce reorder quantities."*
   - **Cash flow & dues outlook** — *"₹6.2L stuck in insurance pipeline > 60 days. Two TPAs (listed) are the bottleneck."*
   - **Top 3 recommended actions for the coming month**, ranked by expected impact.

   This is the kind of analysis a part-time CFO would do for you at ₹40–60k/month. Sitha does it automatically, every month, using data that's already in your HMS.

#### Automatic Instagram posting

Sitha auto-generates and schedules Instagram posts from real hospital activity:

- **Camp recap posts** — after every outreach camp, Sitha drafts a post with the village name, number of screenings, and a celebratory message. No marketing intern needed.
- **Awareness posts** — diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma week, cataract month — auto-scheduled and tailored to your hospital's voice.
- **Patient testimonials** — when a thank-you note comes in, Sitha turns it into a graphic post (with consent).
- **Doctor spotlights** — new joinings, conference presentations, fellowship completions.

**Why this matters:** hospitals that post consistently on Instagram see 20–35% YoY growth in walk-ins from the 25–45 age bracket (the people Googling "best eye hospital near me"). Hiring a social media agency for this costs ₹15–25k/month and they don't know your hospital. Sitha does, and it costs less.

**Why this price:** AI inference, scheduled jobs, and Instagram automation are recurring infra costs for us. The ₹10k/month is what keeps Sitha running. The same setup fee as Basic means you can upgrade later without paying any extra money.

### 🔵 Pro — ₹60,000 setup + ₹15,000 / month

**For:** hospitals where the front desk is the bottleneck — high call volume, missed follow-ups, and lost revenue from un-tracked enquiries. Pro is Premier + a fully integrated call logging system.

**Includes everything in Premier, plus:**

#### Call Logs module

Every incoming and outgoing call is logged, linked to a patient (existing or new), tagged by purpose, and tracked through to outcome. Examples:

1. **No more "I called your hospital yesterday, nobody told me anything"**
   Every call has a timestamp, the staff member who attended, the purpose, the notes, and the next action. Patient calls back? Whoever picks up sees the full history.

2. **Enquiry → appointment conversion tracking**
   You'll finally know: of 240 enquiry calls last month, how many became consults? How many became surgeries? Which staff member converts best? Which call source (Google, Instagram, walk-in referral) converts best?

3. **Follow-up call scheduling**
   Post-op day 7, day 30, 3 months — Sitha and the call log work together. The system tells the front desk who to call today, and the call log captures the outcome.

4. **Recovery of "lost" enquiries**
   Patients who called once and never came in. The call log surfaces them weekly. Calling back 30 such patients with a small offer typically converts 4–6 — that's 4–6 surgeries per month that would otherwise be gone.

5. **Staff accountability**
   Call attended? Yes/no. Average call duration. Calls per shift. Removes the "I didn't get the call" excuse and gives the manager a real picture.

**Why this matters:** for a mid-sized eye hospital, the front desk handles 30–80 calls/day. Even a 5% improvement in conversion is 1–4 extra surgeries per week — typically ₹50k–₹2L in additional revenue per week. The ₹15k/month fee pays for itself many times over in the first month.

**Why this price:** the call log module requires telephony integration, additional storage for call metadata, and higher-touch support during setup. Hence the slightly higher setup (₹60k) and monthly fee. This is the plan for hospitals where every missed call is a missed surgery.

## Upgrade & downgrade

You can upgrade from Basic → Premier or Premier → Pro at any time. We only charge the *difference* in setup fee (if any), prorated. Downgrades take effect at the end of the current billing cycle.

## Payment terms

- Setup fee invoiced on contract signing, payable before go-live
- Recurring fees invoiced at the start of each cycle (every 6 months for Basic, monthly for Premier and Pro)
- Annual prepay on monthly plans = **10% discount**
- All prices exclusive of GST

## How to choose

- **You're moving off paper or a basic billing tool, and you want clear numbers about your hospital** → **Basic**.
- **You already run a tight operation and now want to grow patient volume without hiring a marketing team** → **Premier**.
- **Your front desk handles a lot of calls and you suspect you're losing patients in the funnel** → **Pro**.

When in doubt, start with Basic. You can always upgrade — we'll never make that painful.
